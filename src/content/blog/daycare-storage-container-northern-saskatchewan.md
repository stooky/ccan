Recently, a northern Saskatchewan community found a practical and affordable solution to an ongoing storage challenge at its daycare facility by purchasing a [20-foot used shipping container](/containers/20ft-standard/) from C-Can Sam Container Sales & Rentals in Martensville.

## Meeting a Growing Need

Like many daycare facilities, the center had accumulated a variety of equipment and supplies over the years. Seasonal outdoor toys, maintenance equipment, event supplies, and other essential items were taking up valuable indoor space that could be better utilized for programming and activities.

The daycare needed a secure, weather-resistant storage solution that could withstand Saskatchewan's changing seasons while keeping equipment organized and protected.

After exploring their options, the community chose a 20-foot used shipping container from C-Can Sam.

## A Cost-Effective Storage Solution

For many organizations operating within tight budgets, finding affordable infrastructure solutions is a priority. A quality used shipping container offered the perfect balance of durability, security, and value.

The container provides:

- Secure storage for outdoor play equipment
- Protection from rain, snow, and harsh weather
- Additional space for seasonal supplies
- Improved organization around the daycare property
- Long-term durability with minimal maintenance

The daycare staff were pleased to gain much-needed storage capacity without the expense and construction time associated with building a traditional storage shed.

## Built for Saskatchewan Conditions

One of the biggest advantages of shipping containers is their rugged construction. Originally designed to withstand international shipping and harsh environments, they are well suited for Saskatchewan communities, including those in remote and northern regions.

The steel construction helps protect valuable equipment from the elements while providing a secure location for supplies year-round.

For the daycare, this means toys, equipment, and maintenance items can be stored safely and remain ready for use whenever needed.

From the initial inquiry to final delivery, the goal was simple: provide a dependable storage solution that would meet the daycare's needs for years to come.

<details class="faq-item">
<summary>Will a shipping container survive Saskatchewan winters?</summary>
<div class="faq-answer">
Yes—shipping containers are built to survive extreme conditions on ocean voyages, including arctic routes. They handle temperature swings from -40°C to +50°C without issue. The steel construction resists corrosion, and proper seals keep moisture out, making them ideal for protecting equipment year-round in our prairie climate.
</div>
</details>

<details class="faq-item">
<summary>Why choose a used container instead of building a storage shed?</summary>
<div class="faq-answer">
A used shipping container is typically more affordable than building a traditional shed, arrives ready to use with no construction time, and is far more secure and durable. The steel structure requires minimal maintenance and can last 25+ years in ground storage, making it a smart long-term investment for organizations watching their budget.
</div>
</details>

<details class="faq-item">
<summary>Is a used shipping container secure?</summary>
<div class="faq-answer">
Very. Shipping containers are made from heavy-gauge steel with lockable steel doors, making them one of the most secure storage options available. Many customers add a lock box or additional locking hardware for extra peace of mind, keeping valuable equipment safe year-round.
</div>
</details>

<details class="faq-item">
<summary>What should I look for when buying a used shipping container?</summary>
<div class="faq-answer">
Key things to check: door operation and seals, floor condition (should be solid marine-grade plywood), roof integrity (no holes or heavy rust), and overall structural soundness. Surface rust is normal and cosmetic—deep rust or holes are concerns.
